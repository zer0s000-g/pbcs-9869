# PBCS Institutional Maturity Model

> [!summary] TL;DR
> Where is your organization on the PBCS journey? Use this model to assess your current level, identify gaps, and plan your next steps. Each level maps to specific KB pages and real-world milestones.

---

## The five levels

```
Level 1         Level 2          Level 3           Level 4            Level 5
AWARE          PLANNING        IMPLEMENTING       OPERATIONAL        OPTIMIZING

"We've heard   "We're mapping   "We're building   "We're live       "We're improving
 of PBCS"       our path"       evidence"         and monitoring"   and sharing"
```

## Level 1 — AWARE

**What it looks like:**
Your organization has heard of PBCS and knows it relates to CPDLC and ADS-C, but has not started formal planning.

**Typical state:**
- ☐ Someone attended a regional PBCS workshop
- ☐ Doc 9869 PDF exists somewhere on a shared drive
- ☐ No formal PBCS owner or working group
- ☐ No gap analysis against current operations

**What to do next:**
- Assign a PBCS lead
- Complete the [[Beginner Path]] as a team (15 min)
- Review the [[Quick Reference Card]] — understand the landscape
- Identify which airspace your operations use (do any require PBCS?)

**Key KB pages:**
- [[Beginner Path]]
- [[Quick Reference Card]]
- [[Performance-Based Communication and Surveillance]]
- [[Acronyms and Terms]]

## Level 2 — PLANNING

**What it looks like:**
Your organization has identified that PBCS applies to some or all of its operations and is actively planning implementation.

**Typical state:**
- ☐ PBCS lead assigned with executive sponsorship
- ☐ Airspace/route inventory completed — which require PBCS?
- ☐ Applicable RCP/RSP specifications identified
- ☐ Stakeholder mapping started (which CSP/ANSP/State?)
- ☐ High-level timeline and budget estimated

**What to do next:**
- Confirm the exact RCP/RSP prescribed for your target airspace (AIP check)
- Contact your CSP about data provision clauses
- Begin operator readiness assessment
- Join the relevant regional PBCS coordination group

**Key KB pages:**
- [[Choosing Your RCP-RSP Specification]]
- [[APAC PBCS Regulatory Mapping]] (or your region)
- [[Is Your Aircraft PBCS-Capable]]
- [[Implementation Path]]
- [[Learning Tracks]] — role-specific training plan

## Level 3 — IMPLEMENTING

**What it looks like:**
Your organization is actively building its PBCS evidence package and working toward operational approval.

**Typical state:**
- ☐ RCP/RSP specifications confirmed from State AIP
- ☐ Operator capability assessment complete (per [[Is Your Aircraft PBCS-Capable]])
- ☐ Crew training programme designed and underway
- ☐ MEL updated with CPDLC/ADS-C dispatch relief
- ☐ CSP contract includes data provision clause
- ☐ Flight plan filing procedures updated
- ☐ Monitoring programme design started
- ☐ Corrective action process drafted
- ☐ [[PBCS Compliance Matrix Template]] partially filled

**What to do next:**
- Complete the compliance matrix (see [[PBCS Compliance Worked Example]])
- Schedule ORT (Operational Readiness Trial)
- Submit approval application to State
- Finalize monitoring feed and data collection

## Level 4 — OPERATIONAL

**What it looks like:**
Your organization has State approval, is operating under PBCS, and is running post-implementation monitoring.

**Typical state:**
- ☐ Operational approval received from State authority
- ☐ PBCS operations active in designated airspace
- ☐ Post-implementation monitoring programme running
- ☐ Monthly performance data collected and analyzed
- ☐ Regular reports submitted per State/regional requirements
- ☐ Corrective action process tested (drill or real event)
- ☐ Crew recurrent training includes PBCS
- ☐ New aircraft/crew additions follow established approval path

**What to do next:**
- Analyze the first 6-12 months of monitoring data for trends
- Reduce reporting cadence if performance is stable (per State approval)
- Document lessons learned for internal knowledge base
- Consider expanding PBCS to additional airspace or routes
- Contribute verification passes back to the upstream KB

**Key KB pages:**
- [[Post-Implementation Monitoring]]
- [[Performance Analysis]]
- [[Corrective Action]]

- [[Data Collection]]

## Level 5 — OPTIMIZING

**What it looks like:**
Your organization has mature PBCS operations, stable monitoring, and is actively improving and sharing knowledge.

**Typical state:**
- ☐ Multiple years of stable PBCS operations
- ☐ Monitoring largely automated with exception-based alerting
- ☐ Corrective action response times optimized
- ☐ PBCS expanded to multiple airspace blocks
- ☐ Internal PBCS knowledge base maintained alongside this KB
- ☐ Staff contribute to regional PBCS working groups
- ☐ Lessons shared with industry (conferences, papers, TF participation)
- ☐ Proactive technology evaluation (new satcom providers, ATN transition)

**What to do next:**
- Evaluate emerging technologies (Iridium Certus, VDL Mode 2 expansion, ATN/OSI migration)
- Contribute regulatory mapping and case studies to the upstream KB
- Mentor other States/ANSPs/operators earlier in the maturity curve
- Lead or co-chair regional PBCS working groups
- Explore RCP/RSP specification modernization proposals

**Key KB pages:**

- [[Changelog]] — track your own KB evolution

## Self-assessment quick check

Answer these questions to find your level:

| Question | If YES, you're at least... |
|---|---|
| Does your org have a named PBCS lead? | Level 2 |
| Have you identified which RCP/RSP applies to you? | Level 2 |
| Is your compliance matrix partially filled? | Level 3 |
| Have you submitted an approval application? | Level 3 |
| Do you have State operational approval? | Level 4 |
| Is your monitoring programme running? | Level 4 |
| Have you reduced monitoring frequency after stable performance? | Level 5 |
| Do you contribute to regional PBCS working groups? | Level 5 |

---

> [!tip] Use this as a roadmap
> Don't aim for Level 5 immediately. Each level builds on the previous. Most organizations spend 6-18 months moving from Level 2 to Level 4. The jump from Level 1 to Level 2 can happen in weeks once executive commitment is obtained.
